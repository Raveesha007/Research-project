# Music Studio Pro - Quick Start Guide

## 🚀 Getting Started in 5 Minutes

### Step 1: Open the Application
1. Extract the Music Studio Pro folder to your computer
2. Open `index.html` in a modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge recommended)
3. The professional landing page will load

### Step 2: Grant Microphone Permission
1. Click **"Start Learning"** button
2. Your browser will ask for microphone permission
3. Click **"Allow"** to grant permission
4. ✅ You're ready to learn!

### Step 3: Select Your Instrument
1. Choose **Piano** 🎹 or **Guitar** 🎸
2. Click to select your instrument
3. The system will automatically proceed to the next step

### Step 4: Choose Your Skill Level
1. Select your experience level:
   - **Beginner** - New to this instrument
   - **Intermediate** - Some experience
   - **Advanced** - Solid skills
2. Click your level to continue

### Step 5: Pick a Lesson
1. Browse available lessons with descriptions
2. Click on a lesson that interests you
3. The lesson will load with reference materials

### Step 6: Start Practicing
1. **Read the reference material** on the left side panel
2. Click **"🎤 Start Recording"** to begin
3. **Play or sing** the lesson material into your microphone
4. Click **"⏹ Stop"** when finished
5. **Receive AI feedback** with performance analysis

## 📊 Understanding Your Results

### Performance Metrics
- **Pitch Accuracy %**: How close you are to the target pitch
- **Timing %**: Consistency of your rhythm and timing
- **Rhythm %**: Overall rhythm control and steadiness

### Feedback Indicators
- 🔊 **Clarity**: How clear your audio is
- 🎯 **Pitch Stability**: How steady your notes are
- 💡 **Improvement Tips**: Actionable suggestions

### Score Interpretation
- **90-100%**: Excellent! Keep practicing
- **80-89%**: Very Good! Minor improvements needed
- **70-79%**: Good! More practice recommended
- **Below 70%**: Keep practicing! You'll improve

## 🎯 Tips for Best Results

### Audio Quality
1. Use a quiet environment
2. Keep the microphone close (6-12 inches away)
3. Avoid background noise and music
4. Use a quality microphone if available
5. Ensure your microphone is properly connected

### Recording Tips
1. Warm up before recording
2. Record longer sequences (3+ seconds optimal)
3. Play/sing clearly and distinctly
4. Stay relaxed and confident
5. Record multiple times to compare results

### Practice Schedule
- **15-30 minutes daily** is ideal
- Practice scales and basics first
- Focus on one lesson at a time
- Record yourself to track progress
- Move to next level only when confident

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

### "Microphone not detected"
- ✓ Check browser permissions
- ✓ Ensure microphone is plugged in
- ✓ Test microphone on another application
- ✓ Restart your browser
- ✓ Try a different browser

### "No notes detected"
- ✓ Play/sing louder and clearer
- ✓ Move microphone closer
- ✓ Reduce background noise
- ✓ Record longer sequences
- ✓ Try again - sometimes first attempt needs retry

### "Poor audio quality"
- ✓ Use a better microphone if available
- ✓ Close other applications using audio
- ✓ Move away from fans/AC units
- ✓ Minimize network activity
- ✓ Check microphone input levels

### "Low confidence feedback"
- ✓ The system couldn't clearly hear you
- ✓ Record again with clearer audio
- ✓ Play notes individually not together
- ✓ Ensure steady, consistent tone

## 🎵 Music Theory Quick Reference

### Note Frequencies
- C = 261.63 Hz
- D = 293.66 Hz
- E = 329.63 Hz
- F = 349.23 Hz
- G = 392.00 Hz
- A = 440.00 Hz (Standard)
- B = 493.88 Hz

### Octave Ranges
- **Low Range**: A0 to B2
- **Middle Range**: C3 to B5
- **High Range**: C6 to B8

### For Pianists
- White keys = Natural notes (C, D, E, F, G, A, B)
- Black keys = Sharp/Flat notes (C#, D#, etc.)
- 88 total keys on professional piano
- Middle C = C4

### For Guitarists
- 6 strings: E, A, D, G, B, E (low to high)
- Fretboard has 20-24 frets typically
- Standard tuning used in this system
- Capos can be simulated in lessons
